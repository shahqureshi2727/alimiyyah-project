# Fiqh-22 — Vehicles and Travel

## Topic: Prayer on Animals and Vehicles  #fiqh/vehicles

- **[FQH-VEH-01]** Farḍ and wājib prayers are invalid when performed on a riding animal or in a
  moving vehicle. · src: Fiqh-22 "Fardh and Wajib Prayers" ^FQH-VEH-01
- **[FQH-VEH-02]** The same ruling of invalidity while riding applies to Sajdah al-Tilāwah, if the
  verse requiring it was recited or heard before mounting. · src: Fiqh-22 "Fardh and Wajib
  Prayers" ^FQH-VEH-02
- **[FQH-VEH-03]** Praying farḍ/wājib prayers on a riding animal or moving vehicle is excused out
  of necessity — e.g. muddy terrain, fear of a thief or danger, or inability to remount after
  descending. · src: Fiqh-22 "Fardh and Wajib Prayers" ^FQH-VEH-03
- **[FQH-VEH-04]** If a carriage mounted on a camel is made stationary and connected to the ground
  (e.g. by a wooden support), one may pray standing inside it, as it takes the legal ruling of
  being on the ground. · src: Fiqh-22 "Stationary Carriage" ^FQH-VEH-04
- **[FQH-VEH-05]** Outside city limits, a traveller may pray voluntary (nafl) prayers and the
  emphasized sunnah prayers on an animal or vehicle, performing the Ṣalāh through head movements.
  · src: Fiqh-22 "Voluntary Prayers While Traveling" ^FQH-VEH-05
- **[FQH-VEH-06]** Facing the qiblah throughout the prayer is not required for a traveller praying
  nafl/sunnah prayers on an animal or vehicle, under the travel concession. · src: Fiqh-22
  "Voluntary Prayers While Traveling" ^FQH-VEH-06
- **[FQH-VEH-07]** Filth on the saddle or stirrups of a riding animal is excused for a traveller
  praying upon it. · src: Fiqh-22 "Voluntary Prayers While Traveling" ^FQH-VEH-07
- **[FQH-VEH-08]** Performing ritual prayer while walking is not valid; one must stop and
  establish the prayer in a valid manner. · src: Fiqh-22 "Salaah While Walking" ^FQH-VEH-08
- **[FQH-VEH-09]** For nafl prayers, one may pray sitting even when capable of standing. · src:
  Fiqh-22 "Salaah While Walking" ^FQH-VEH-09
- **[FQH-VEH-10]** Prayer performed while sitting on a ship is valid, even without an excuse,
  provided the worshipper turns toward the qiblah every time the ship changes direction. · src:
  Fiqh-22 "Praying on a Ship" ^FQH-VEH-10

## Topic: Travel (Safar)  #fiqh/travel

- **[FQH-TRV-01]** Travel rulings apply once a person leaves their place of residence intending to
  travel to a destination requiring at least three days' travel at a moderate pace; at that
  point, they become a traveller (musāfir). · src: Fiqh-22 "Definition of Travel" ^FQH-TRV-01
- **[FQH-TRV-02]** A traveller may delay fasting in Ramaḍān and make it up later. · src: Fiqh-22
  "Concessions for the Traveller" ^FQH-TRV-02
- **[FQH-TRV-03]** A traveller may shorten (qaṣr) the four-rakʿah obligatory prayers — Ẓuhr, ʿAṣr,
  and ʿIshāʾ — to two rakʿahs each. · src: Fiqh-22 "Concessions for the Traveller" ^FQH-TRV-03
- **[FQH-TRV-04]** Fajr and Maghrib are never shortened, even for a traveller. · src: Fiqh-22
  "Concessions for the Traveller" ^FQH-TRV-04
- **[FQH-TRV-05]** A traveller becomes a resident again once they reach a city or village and
  intend to remain there for 15 full days or more. · src: Fiqh-22 "When Travel Rulings End"
  ^FQH-TRV-05
- **[FQH-TRV-06]** Once a traveller becomes a resident again, the four-rakʿah Ṣalāh must be prayed
  in full, and fasting in Ramaḍān is required. · src: Fiqh-22 "When Travel Rulings End"
  ^FQH-TRV-06
- **[FQH-TRV-07]** If a traveller intends to stay somewhere fewer than 15 days, they remain a
  traveller — qaṣr continues, and the choice regarding fasting remains available to them. · src:
  Fiqh-22 "Returning Home" ^FQH-TRV-07
- **[FQH-TRV-08]** Travel rulings end when the traveller enters the limits of their permanent
  residence. · src: Fiqh-22 "Returning Home" ^FQH-TRV-08
