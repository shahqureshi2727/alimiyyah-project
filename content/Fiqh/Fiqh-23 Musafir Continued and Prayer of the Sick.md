# Fiqh-23 — Musafir (Continued); Prayer of the Sick

## Topic: Travel (Safar)  #fiqh/travel

- **[FQH-TRV-09]** If a traveller prays behind a resident imam during the prayer's valid time,
  the traveller follows the imam and performs the full four rakʿahs, not shortened. · src:
  Fiqh-23 "Following an Imam" ^FQH-TRV-09
- **[FQH-TRV-10]** If a traveller prays behind a resident imam after the prayer's time has ended,
  that Ṣalāh is invalid. · src: Fiqh-23 "Following an Imam" ^FQH-TRV-10
- **[FQH-TRV-11]** A resident may pray behind a travelling imam; when the traveller-imam completes
  his shortened prayer after two rakʿahs, the resident stands and completes the remaining two
  rakʿahs himself. · src: Fiqh-23 "Resident Following a Traveller" ^FQH-TRV-11
- **[FQH-TRV-12]** If a travelling imam mistakenly performs a full four rakʿahs and a resident
  follows him throughout, the resident's farḍ Ṣalāh becomes invalid and counts only as a
  voluntary prayer. · src: Fiqh-23 "Resident Following a Traveller" ^FQH-TRV-12
- **[FQH-TRV-13]** The status of any Ṣalāh, for qaḍāʾ purposes, is based on when it originally
  became farḍ upon the person. · src: Fiqh-23 "Qada of Missed Prayers" ^FQH-TRV-13
- **[FQH-TRV-14]** A four-rakʿah Ṣalāh missed while travelling is made up (qaḍāʾ) as two rakʿahs,
  even if the make-up prayer is performed after returning home. · src: Fiqh-23 "Qada of Missed
  Prayers" ^FQH-TRV-14
- **[FQH-TRV-15]** A four-rakʿah Ṣalāh missed while resident is made up as four rakʿahs, even if
  the make-up prayer is performed while travelling. · src: Fiqh-23 "Qada of Missed Prayers"
  ^FQH-TRV-15
- **[FQH-TRV-16]** The ruling on how many rakʿahs to make up is based on the person's status
  (resident or traveller) at the end of the original prayer's time, not at the time of making it
  up. · src: Fiqh-23 "Qada of Missed Prayers" ^FQH-TRV-16
- **[FQH-TRV-17]** A traveller on a permissible journey and a traveller on a journey involving
  disobedience are treated equally regarding travel concessions, including shortening the prayer
  (qaṣr) and the option not to fast during Ramaḍān. · src: Fiqh-23 "Travel for Sinful Purposes"
  ^FQH-TRV-17
- **[FQH-TRV-18]** The sinfulness of a journey does not remove the legal rulings/concessions of
  travel. · src: Fiqh-23 "Travel for Sinful Purposes" ^FQH-TRV-18

## Topic: Prayer of the Sick Person  #fiqh/prayer-of-the-sick

- **[FQH-MRD-01]** A person may pray seated if standing is difficult or harmful due to severe
  pain, fear that the illness will worsen, or fear that recovery will be delayed. · src: Fiqh-23
  "Prayer of the Sick Person — If a Person Cannot Stand" ^FQH-MRD-01
- **[FQH-MRD-02]** A person praying seated performs the normal bowing (rukūʿ) and prostration
  (sujūd), and may sit in any comfortable manner. · src: Fiqh-23 "Prayer of the Sick Person"
  ^FQH-MRD-02
- **[FQH-MRD-03]** If a sick person can stand for only part of the Ṣalāh, they must stand for as
  much of it as they are able. · src: Fiqh-23 "Prayer of the Sick Person" ^FQH-MRD-03
- **[FQH-MRD-04]** A person who cannot bow or prostrate should pray using head gestures, with the
  gesture for sujūd made lower than the gesture for rukūʿ. · src: Fiqh-23 "Prayer of the Sick
  Person — If a Person Cannot Bow or Prostrate" ^FQH-MRD-04
- **[FQH-MRD-05]** The Ṣalāh is not valid if the gesture used for sujūd is the same as the gesture
  used for rukūʿ. · src: Fiqh-23 "Prayer of the Sick Person" ^FQH-MRD-05
- **[FQH-MRD-06]** A sick person performs whatever they are able of the Qurʾān recitation,
  tasbīḥ, tashahhud, and other adhkār of the prayer; whatever they are unable to perform is
  waived. · src: Fiqh-23 "Other Actions of Salaah" ^FQH-MRD-06
- **[FQH-MRD-07]** If sitting becomes too difficult, a person should pray lying on their back
  (preferred) or on their side. · src: Fiqh-23 "If Sitting Is Difficult" ^FQH-MRD-07
- **[FQH-MRD-08]** The recommended position for a person praying lying down is: a pillow or
  support placed under the head, facing the qiblah rather than the sky, gesturing with the head
  for rukūʿ and sujūd, and keeping the knees bent where possible rather than stretching the legs
  toward the qiblah. · src: Fiqh-23 "Recommended Position" ^FQH-MRD-08
- **[FQH-MRD-09]** If a person cannot even gesture with their head, the Ṣalāh is delayed — they do
  not gesture with their eyes, eyebrows, or heart instead, since the obligation of rukūʿ and sujūd
  is not fulfilled by such actions. · src: Fiqh-23 "If Even Head Gestures Are Impossible"
  ^FQH-MRD-09
- **[FQH-MRD-10]** If the inability to pray lasts one day and night or less, and the person
  recovers, they must make up only those missed prayers; if they die in that state, no sin or
  liability remains upon them. · src: Fiqh-23 "Making Up Missed Salaah" ^FQH-MRD-10
- **[FQH-MRD-11]** If the inability to pray lasts long enough to miss more than five prayers, the
  obligation of making up those missed prayers is waived entirely, to remove undue hardship from
  the sick person. · src: Fiqh-23 "If the Inability Lasts More [than] 5 Salaah" ^FQH-MRD-11
- **[FQH-MRD-12]** No fidyah is required if a sick person dies before recovering sufficiently to
  make up missed prayers or fasts, or if a traveller dies before returning and having the
  opportunity to make up missed fasts. · src: Fiqh-23 "Fidyah for Missed Prayers and Fasts — When
  No Fidyah Is Required" ^FQH-MRD-12
- **[FQH-MRD-13]** Fidyah is required if a sick person recovers, or a traveller returns and
  resides for at least fifteen days, yet dies before making up the missed prayers or fasts — in
  which case a bequest for fidyah should be made from up to one-third of the estate. · src:
  Fiqh-23 "Fidyah for Missed Prayers and Fasts — When Fidyah Is Required" ^FQH-MRD-13
- **[FQH-MRD-14]** The fidyah amount for each missed prayer (including witr) and each missed fast
  is half a ṣāʿ (approximately 2.2kg) of wheat, or one ṣāʿ (approximately 4.4kg) of barley, or
  their cash equivalent. · src: Fiqh-23 "Fidyah for Missed Prayers and Fasts" ^FQH-MRD-14

## Flags
