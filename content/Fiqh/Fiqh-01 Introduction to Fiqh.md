# Fiqh-01 — Introduction to Fiqh

## Topic: Introduction to Fiqh  #fiqh/introduction

- **[FQH-INT-01]** Fiqh (فِقْه) is the science of understanding and applying Islamic law as
  derived from detailed evidence within the foundational texts of Islam — in practice, Islamic
  law applied to daily life. · src: Fiqh-01 "What is Fiqh?" ^FQH-INT-01
- **[FQH-INT-02]** The two primary sources of Islamic law are the Qurʾān and the Sunnah. ·
  src: Fiqh-01 "Primary Sources" ^FQH-INT-02
- **[FQH-INT-03]** The two secondary sources of Islamic law are Ijmāʿ (إِجْمَاع, scholarly
  consensus) and Qiyās (قِيَاس, analogical reasoning). · src: Fiqh-01 "Secondary Sources"
  ^FQH-INT-03
- **[FQH-INT-04]** Farḍ (فَرْض) is the ruling category for an obligatory act. · src: Fiqh-01
  "Types of rulings" ^FQH-INT-04
- **[FQH-INT-05]** Sunnah (سُنَّة), as a ruling category, is a highly emphasised act. · src:
  Fiqh-01 "Types of rulings" ^FQH-INT-05
- **[FQH-INT-06]** Mustaḥabb (مُسْتَحَبّ) is the ruling category for a recommended act. · src:
  Fiqh-01 "Types of rulings" ^FQH-INT-06
- **[FQH-INT-07]** Mubāḥ (مُبَاح) is the ruling category for a permissible act. · src: Fiqh-01
  "Types of rulings" ^FQH-INT-07
- **[FQH-INT-08]** Ḥarām (حَرَام) is the ruling category for an impermissible act. · src: Fiqh-01
  "Types of rulings" ^FQH-INT-08
- **[FQH-INT-09]** A Faqīh (فَقِيه, jurist) derives Islamic rulings (the "final product") from the
  sources of Islamic law (the "ingredients") — analogous to a baker producing bread from
  ingredients. · src: Fiqh-01 "Ingredients / Final product / Baker" ^FQH-INT-09
- **[FQH-INT-10]** Farḍ ʿAyn (فَرْض عَيْن) is an obligation every individually responsible Muslim
  must personally fulfil; no one else can fulfil it on their behalf. · src: Fiqh-01 "How much
  Fiqh is Fardh?" ^FQH-INT-10
- **[FQH-INT-11]** Farḍ Kifāyah (فَرْض كِفَايَة) is an obligation placed on the Muslim community as
  a whole; if enough people fulfil it, the rest of the community is absolved of it. · src:
  Fiqh-01 "How much Fiqh is Fardh?" ^FQH-INT-11
