# Fiqh-14 — Prohibited/Disliked Prayer Times; Adhan & Iqamah

## Topic: Salah Prayer Times  #fiqh/prayer-times

- **[FQH-SLH-12]** Ṣalāh is prohibited at sunrise. · src: Fiqh-14 "Prohibited Times" ^FQH-SLH-12
- **[FQH-SLH-13]** Ṣalāh is prohibited at midday (istiwāʾ/zenith). · src: Fiqh-14 "Prohibited
  Times" ^FQH-SLH-13
- **[FQH-SLH-14]** Ṣalāh is prohibited at sunset, except that a day's ʿAṣr prayer may still be
  completed at sunset if it has not yet been prayed. · src: Fiqh-14 "Prohibited Times"
  ^FQH-SLH-14
- **[FQH-SLH-15]** It is disliked (makrūh) to pray during the khuṭbah (sermon). · src: Fiqh-14
  "Disliked Times for Salah" ^FQH-SLH-15
- **[FQH-SLH-16]** Voluntary prayer is disliked before Fajr (aside from the two rakʿahs of the
  Fajr sunnah). · src: Fiqh-14 "Disliked Times for Salah" ^FQH-SLH-16
- **[FQH-SLH-17]** Voluntary prayer is disliked after performing the Fajr prayer, continuing until
  after sunrise. · src: Fiqh-14 "Disliked Times for Salah" ^FQH-SLH-17
- **[FQH-SLH-18]** Voluntary prayer is disliked after performing the ʿAṣr prayer, continuing until
  sunset. · src: Fiqh-14 "Disliked Times for Salah" ^FQH-SLH-18
- **[FQH-SLH-19]** Voluntary prayer is disliked at the time of the iqāmah. · src: Fiqh-14
  "Disliked Times for Salah" ^FQH-SLH-19
- **[FQH-SLH-20]** Voluntary prayer is disliked before and after the ʿĪd prayer while at the
  muṣallā (prayer ground). · src: Fiqh-14 "Disliked Times for Salah" ^FQH-SLH-20
- **[FQH-SLH-21]** Prayer is disliked when the time remaining for an obligatory prayer is short. ·
  src: Fiqh-14 "Disliked Times for Salah" ^FQH-SLH-21
- **[FQH-SLH-22]** Prayer is disliked at meal time if one strongly desires the food that has been
  served. · src: Fiqh-14 "Disliked Times for Salah" ^FQH-SLH-22
- **[FQH-SLH-23]** Prayer is disliked when something distracts the person, preventing full focus
  and breaking concentration during the prayer. · src: Fiqh-14 "Disliked Times for Salah"
  ^FQH-SLH-23

## Topic: Adhan and Iqamah  #fiqh/adhan

- **[FQH-ADH-01]** The Adhan and Iqāmah are emphasized sunnahs (sunnah muʾakkadah), applicable for
  the five daily prayers and the Friday (Jumuʿah) prayer. · src: Fiqh-14 "Rulings of Adhan"
  ^FQH-ADH-01
- **[FQH-ADH-02]** The 15-phrase Adhan consists of: Allāhu Akbar said four times, the two
  testimonies of faith (shahādatayn) said twice each, "Ḥayya ʿalā al-Ṣalāh" and "Ḥayya ʿalā
  al-Falāḥ" said twice each, Allāhu Akbar said twice more, and "Lā ilāha illa Allāh" said once at
  the end. · src: Fiqh-14 "15 Phrase Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-02
- **[FQH-ADH-03]** The 17-phrase Adhan variant begins with only two opening takbīrs, follows with
  the shahādatayn said quietly twice each and then aloud twice each, then continues as the
  standard adhan (the "Ḥayya ʿalā" phrases, closing takbīrs, and the final tahlīl). · src: Fiqh-14
  "17 Phrase Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-03
- **[FQH-ADH-04]** The 19-phrase Adhan variant keeps the standard four opening takbīrs, adds the
  shahādatayn said quietly twice each before repeating them aloud twice each, then continues as
  the standard adhan. · src: Fiqh-14 "19 Phrase Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-04
- **[FQH-ADH-05]** The mu'adhin (caller to prayer) must be truthful and know the prayer timings. ·
  src: Fiqh-14 "Requirments for Mua'dhin" ^FQH-ADH-05
- **[FQH-ADH-06]** The mu'adhin must have wuḍūʾ and face the qiblah while calling the adhan. ·
  src: Fiqh-14 "Requirments for Mua'dhin" ^FQH-ADH-06
- **[FQH-ADH-07]** The mu'adhin places his index fingers in his ears, or his hands over his ears,
  while calling the adhan. · src: Fiqh-14 "Requirments for Mua'dhin" ^FQH-ADH-07
- **[FQH-ADH-08]** The mu'adhin turns his head to the right and left when saying "Ḥayya ʿalā
  al-Ṣalāh" and "Ḥayya ʿalā al-Falāḥ" respectively. · src: Fiqh-14 "Requirments for Mua'dhin"
  ^FQH-ADH-08
- **[FQH-ADH-09]** The adhan is recited slowly with pauses, while the iqāmah is recited quickly. ·
  src: Fiqh-14 "Requirments for Mua'dhin" ^FQH-ADH-09
- **[FQH-ADH-10]** It is recommended (sunnah) for the mu'adhin, during cold weather or rainy
  nights, to add after the two "Ḥayya ʿalā" phrases: "Ṣallū fī riḥālikum" (صَلُّوا فِي رِحَالِكُمْ,
  "Pray in your dwellings") or "Ṣallū fī buyūtikum" (صَلُّوا فِي بُيُوتِكُمْ, "Pray in your
  homes"). · src: Fiqh-14 "Requirments for Mua'dhin" ^FQH-ADH-10
- **[FQH-ADH-11]** The listener of the adhan should listen to it attentively. · src: Fiqh-14
  "Listener of Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-11
- **[FQH-ADH-12]** The listener of the adhan should repeat after the caller (the dhikr/response to
  each phrase). · src: Fiqh-14 "Listener of Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-12
- **[FQH-ADH-13]** After the adhan, the listener should send salutations (ṣalawāt) upon the
  Prophet ﷺ, make the narrated sunnah duʿāʾ, and make a personal duʿāʾ. · src: Fiqh-14 "Listener
  of Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-13
- **[FQH-ADH-14]** The 10-phrase Iqāmah consists of: Allāhu Akbar said twice, the two testimonies
  of faith said once each, "Ḥayya ʿalā al-Ṣalāh" and "Ḥayya ʿalā al-Falāḥ" said once each, "Qad
  qāmat al-Ṣalāh" said once, Allāhu Akbar said twice more, and "Lā ilāha illa Allāh" said once at
  the end. · src: Fiqh-14 "10 Phrase Iqama" ^FQH-ADH-14
- **[FQH-ADH-15]** The 11-phrase Iqāmah variant is identical to the 10-phrase Iqāmah except that
  "Qad qāmat al-Ṣalāh" is said twice instead of once. · src: Fiqh-14 "11 Phrase Iqama" ^FQH-ADH-15
- **[FQH-ADH-16]** The 17-word Iqāmah says every phrase twice, as in the adhan — the opening
  takbīrs, both testimonies of faith, the "Ḥayya ʿalā" phrases, and "Qad qāmat al-Ṣalāh" —
  followed by two closing takbīrs and the final tahlīl once. · src: Fiqh-14 "17 Word Iqama"
  ^FQH-ADH-16
- **[FQH-ADH-17]** It is recommended to call the adhan at the birth of a newborn. · src: Fiqh-14
  "Recommended Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-17
- **[FQH-ADH-18]** It is recommended to call the adhan during a fire. · src: Fiqh-14 "Recommended
  Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-18
- **[FQH-ADH-19]** It is recommended to call the adhan at the onset of war. · src: Fiqh-14
  "Recommended Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-19
- **[FQH-ADH-20]** It is recommended to call the adhan when jinn cause mischief. · src: Fiqh-14
  "Recommended Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-20
- **[FQH-ADH-21]** It is recommended to call the adhan for someone who is possessed or afflicted. ·
  src: Fiqh-14 "Recommended Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-21
- **[FQH-ADH-22]** It is recommended to call the adhan for someone who is anxious or worried. ·
  src: Fiqh-14 "Recommended Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-22
- **[FQH-ADH-23]** It is recommended to call the adhan for someone who is angry. · src: Fiqh-14
  "Recommended Adhan" ^FQH-ADH-23
