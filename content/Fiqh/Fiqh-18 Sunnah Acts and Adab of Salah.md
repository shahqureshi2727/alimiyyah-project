# Fiqh-18 — Sunnah Acts and Adab of Salah

## Topic: Salah Prayer Times  #fiqh/prayer-times

- **[FQH-SLH-55]** It is sunnah to raise the hands at Takbīr al-Taḥrīmah — men to their ears,
  women to their shoulders. · src: Fiqh-18 "Sunnah Acts" ^FQH-SLH-55
- **[FQH-SLH-56]** It is sunnah to keep the fingers slightly spaced when raising the hands for
  Takbīr al-Taḥrīmah. · src: Fiqh-18 "Sunnah Acts" ^FQH-SLH-56
- **[FQH-SLH-57]** It is sunnah to place the right hand over the left after Takbīr al-Taḥrīmah —
  for men, below the navel; for women, on the chest. · src: Fiqh-18 "Sunnah Acts" ^FQH-SLH-57
- **[FQH-SLH-58]** It is sunnah to recite the opening supplication (Thanāʾ). · src: Fiqh-18
  "Sunnah Acts" ^FQH-SLH-58
- **[FQH-SLH-59]** It is sunnah to recite Taʿawwudh, in the first rakʿah only. · src: Fiqh-18
  "Sunnah Acts" ^FQH-SLH-59
- **[FQH-SLH-60]** It is sunnah to recite the Basmalah before al-Fātiḥah in every rakʿah, except
  for a follower praying behind an imam. · src: Fiqh-18 "Sunnah Acts" ^FQH-SLH-60
- **[FQH-SLH-61]** It is sunnah to say "Āmīn" after al-Fātiḥah. · src: Fiqh-18 "Sunnah Acts"
  ^FQH-SLH-61
- **[FQH-SLH-62]** It is sunnah to say "Rabbanā laka al-ḥamd" after rising from rukūʿ. · src:
  Fiqh-18 "Sunnah Acts" ^FQH-SLH-62
- **[FQH-SLH-63]** The Thanāʾ, Taʿawwudh, Basmalah, Āmīn, and Tahmīd ("Rabbanā laka al-ḥamd") are
  all recited silently. · src: Fiqh-18 "Sunnah Acts" ^FQH-SLH-63
- **[FQH-SLH-64]** It is sunnah for the imam to say the takbīrs aloud. · src: Fiqh-18 "Sunnah
  Acts" ^FQH-SLH-64
- **[FQH-SLH-65]** It is sunnah to say the takbīr when going into rukūʿ, going into sujūd, and
  rising from sujūd. · src: Fiqh-18 "Sunnah Acts" ^FQH-SLH-65
- **[FQH-SLH-66]** It is sunnah to recite the tasbīḥ three times in rukūʿ and in sujūd. · src:
  Fiqh-18 "Sunnah Acts" ^FQH-SLH-66
- **[FQH-SLH-67]** It is sunnah to recite al-Fātiḥah alone, without an additional sūrah, in the
  last two rakʿahs of a four-rakʿah prayer, and in the third rakʿah of Maghrib. · src: Fiqh-18
  "Sunnah Acts" ^FQH-SLH-67
- **[FQH-SLH-68]** It is adab (etiquette) for men to remove their hands from loose sleeves when
  saying Takbīr al-Taḥrīmah. · src: Fiqh-18 "Adab (Etiquette) of Salah" ^FQH-SLH-68
- **[FQH-SLH-69]** The adab for the focus of one's gaze in Ṣalāh is: the place of sujūd while
  standing, the top of the feet in rukūʿ, the tip of the nose in sujūd, the lap while sitting, and
  toward each shoulder during the salām. · src: Fiqh-18 "Adab (Etiquette) of Salah" ^FQH-SLH-69
- **[FQH-SLH-70]** It is adab to maintain composure and stillness in Ṣalāh — avoiding coughing as
  much as possible and keeping the mouth closed when yawning. · src: Fiqh-18 "Adab (Etiquette) of
  Salah" ^FQH-SLH-70
