# Fiqh-21 — Permissible Actions; Breaking/Abandoning the Prayer

## Topic: Salah Prayer Times  #fiqh/prayer-times

- **[FQH-SLH-118]** It is mustaḥabb to place a sutrah (barrier) in front of oneself while
  praying. · src: Fiqh-21 "Permissible in the Prayer" ^FQH-SLH-118
- **[FQH-SLH-119]** If someone attempts to pass in front of a worshipper, it is permissible for
  the worshipper to say "Subḥān Allāh" (for men) or gesture to prevent them from passing. · src:
  Fiqh-21 "Permissible in the Prayer" ^FQH-SLH-119
- **[FQH-SLH-120]** A woman will ward off someone attempting to pass in front of her without
  raising her voice. · src: Fiqh-21 "Permissible in the Prayer" ^FQH-SLH-120
- **[FQH-SLH-121]** Wearing a belt around the waist during Ṣalāh is permissible. · src: Fiqh-21
  "Permissible in the Prayer" ^FQH-SLH-121
- **[FQH-SLH-122]** Carrying a sword or similar item during Ṣalāh is permissible, provided it is
  not distracting. · src: Fiqh-21 "Permissible in the Prayer" ^FQH-SLH-122
- **[FQH-SLH-123]** Praying while facing a copy of the Qurʾān, a sword, the back of a seated
  person (even if they are quietly speaking), or a candle or lamp is permissible. · src: Fiqh-21
  "Permissible in the Prayer" ^FQH-SLH-123
- **[FQH-SLH-124]** Killing a snake or scorpion during Ṣalāh, if harm is feared, is permissible
  with no more than two strikes, even if this requires a slight movement away from the qiblah. ·
  src: Fiqh-21 "Permissible in the Prayer" ^FQH-SLH-124
- **[FQH-SLH-125]** Praying on carpets, rugs, or similar coverings is permissible. · src: Fiqh-21
  "Permissible in the Prayer" ^FQH-SLH-125
- **[FQH-SLH-126]** It is permissible to break the Ṣalāh to protect property of value, even if it
  belongs to someone else. · src: Fiqh-21 "Breaking the Prayer — Permissible to Break" ^FQH-SLH-126
- **[FQH-SLH-127]** A shepherd may break his Ṣalāh if a wolf threatens his flock. · src: Fiqh-21
  "Breaking the Prayer — Permissible to Break" ^FQH-SLH-127
- **[FQH-SLH-128]** It is wājib to break the Ṣalāh when responding to a genuine emergency or call
  for help, if there is reasonable fear that someone may be seriously harmed — such as a blind
  person falling into a well, or similar situations of imminent danger. · src: Fiqh-21 "Breaking
  the Prayer — Obligatory (Wajib) to Break the Prayer" ^FQH-SLH-128
- **[FQH-SLH-129]** A midwife may delay the prayer, even until after its time, if concerned for
  the safety of the mother or baby during childbirth. · src: Fiqh-21 "Delaying Prayer Due to
  Necessity" ^FQH-SLH-129
- **[FQH-SLH-130]** The same ruling — permission to delay prayer past its time — applies to a
  traveller who fears attack from highway robbers. · src: Fiqh-21 "Delaying Prayer Due to
  Necessity" ^FQH-SLH-130
- **[FQH-SLH-131]** A person who intentionally abandons the prayer out of laziness is subject to
  disciplinary punishment by the Islamic authority — beaten until he bleeds, then imprisoned —
  until he resumes praying. · src: Fiqh-21 "Abandoning the Prayer" ^FQH-SLH-131
- **[FQH-SLH-132]** The same disciplinary ruling that applies to one who abandons prayer out of
  laziness also applies to one who does not fast in Ramaḍān. · src: Fiqh-21 "Abandoning the
  Prayer" ^FQH-SLH-132
