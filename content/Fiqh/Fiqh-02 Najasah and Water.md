# Fiqh-02 — Najasah and Water

## Topic: Najasah (Impurities)  #fiqh/najasah

- **[FQH-NJS-01]** Najāsah Ḥaqīqiyyah (نَجَاسَة حَقِيقِيَّة, real/physical impurity) refers to
  physically unclean substances; common examples include urine (of humans or most animals),
  feces, flowing blood, and alcohol. · src: Fiqh-02 "Types of Najasah (Impurities)" ^FQH-NJS-01
- **[FQH-NJS-02]** Najāsah Ḥukmiyyah (نَجَاسَة حُكْمِيَّة, ritual/state-based impurity) is a state
  of ritual impurity rather than physical filth, and includes minor ritual impurity and major
  ritual impurity. · src: Fiqh-02 "Types of Najasah (Impurities)" ^FQH-NJS-02
- **[FQH-NJS-03]** Minor ritual impurity (al-ḥadath al-aṣghar) is a state that prevents a person
  from praying until they perform wuḍūʾ. · src: Fiqh-02 "Najasa Hukmiyya" ^FQH-NJS-03
- **[FQH-NJS-04]** Major ritual impurity (al-ḥadath al-akbar / janābah) is a state that prevents
  prayer, touching the Qurʾān, and other acts until ghusl is performed. · src: Fiqh-02 "Najasa
  Hukmiyya" ^FQH-NJS-04

## Topic: Water Classification  #fiqh/water

- **[FQH-WTR-01]** Water is classified into three categories: pure & purifying (ṭāhir muṭahhir),
  pure but not purifying (ṭāhir ghayr muṭahhir), and impure (najis). · src: Fiqh-02 "Water"
  ^FQH-WTR-01
- **[FQH-WTR-02]** Water remains pure & purifying if only one of its three qualities — colour,
  smell, taste (CST) — changes, any pure substance added to it does not overwhelm it, and it
  remains fluid and recognisable as water. · src: Fiqh-02 "Pure & Purifying Water" ^FQH-WTR-02
- **[FQH-WTR-03]** Rainwater, well water, spring water, river water, sea water, and melted snow
  or hail are all examples of pure & purifying water. · src: Fiqh-02 "Pure & Purifying Water"
  ^FQH-WTR-03
- **[FQH-WTR-04]** Water becomes pure but not purifying when 2 of its 3 qualities (colour, smell,
  taste) change, or it is dominated by another substance, or its fluidity/consistency changes
  (e.g. thickened by food, spices, or mixtures). · src: Fiqh-02 "Pure but not Purifying"
  ^FQH-WTR-04
- **[FQH-WTR-05]** Used water (al-māʾ al-mustaʿmal, اَلْمَاءُ الْمُسْتَعْمَل) — water already used
  to lift ritual impurity via wuḍūʾ or ghusl — is pure but not purifying; it can remove najāsah
  ḥaqīqiyyah but cannot lift ritual impurity again. · src: Fiqh-02 "Pure but Not Purifying"
  ^FQH-WTR-05
- **[FQH-WTR-06]** Water becomes impure (najis) if najāsah falls into it and changes ONE of its
  three qualities: colour, taste, or smell. · src: Fiqh-02 "Impure water" ^FQH-WTR-06
- **[FQH-WTR-07]** If najāsah falls into water but none of its three qualities (colour, taste,
  smell) change, the ruling on whether it is impure depends on the quantity of water. · src:
  Fiqh-02 "When Najasah Falls In but no Change Occurs" ^FQH-WTR-07
- **[FQH-WTR-08]** A small amount of water (e.g. a small container, bucket, or small puddle)
  becomes impure from any amount of najāsah falling into it, even without any visible change. ·
  src: Fiqh-02 "Small Amount of Water" ^FQH-WTR-08
- **[FQH-WTR-09]** `[madhhab-specific]` In the Ḥanafī view, a large amount of water is water such
  that, if one side of it is disturbed, the movement does not reach the other side; such water is
  not rendered impure by unnoticed najāsah. · src: Fiqh-02 "Large amount (Hanafi)" ^FQH-WTR-09
- **[FQH-WTR-10]** `[madhhab-specific]` In the Shāfiʿī view, a large amount of water is defined
  quantitatively as two qullah (qullatayn, قُلَّتَيْن), approximately 200 litres. · src: Fiqh-02
  "Large amount (Shafi)" ^FQH-WTR-10
- **[FQH-WTR-11]** `[madhhab-specific]` In the Mālikī view, the quantity of water (large or
  small) does not matter; the only determining factor for impurity is whether the water's
  colour, taste, or smell changes. · src: Fiqh-02 "Large amount (Maliki)" ^FQH-WTR-11
