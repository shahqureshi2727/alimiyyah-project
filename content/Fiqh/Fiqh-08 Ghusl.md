# Fiqh-08 — Ghusl (Ritual Bath)

## Topic: Ghusl  #fiqh/ghusl

- **[FQH-GHS-01]** Ghusl (ritual bath) is classified into three types: obligatory (farḍ),
  mandatory (wājib), and sunnah/mandūb (recommended). · src: Fiqh-08 "Types of Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-01
- **[FQH-GHS-02]** Ghusl becomes farḍ upon the emission of sperm/sexual fluid (manī) that exits
  its normal place with pleasure. · src: Fiqh-08 "Fardh Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-02
- **[FQH-GHS-03]** Ghusl becomes farḍ upon penetration — the disappearance of the head of the male
  organ into the front or rear private part — even without emission. · src: Fiqh-08 "Fardh Ghusl"
  ^FQH-GHS-03
- **[FQH-GHS-04]** Ghusl becomes farḍ upon the end of menstruation (ḥayḍ). · src: Fiqh-08 "Fardh
  Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-04
- **[FQH-GHS-05]** Ghusl becomes farḍ upon the end of postnatal bleeding (nifās). · src: Fiqh-08
  "Fardh Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-05
- **[FQH-GHS-06]** The exit of madhī or wadī does not require ghusl. · src: Fiqh-08 "Cases Where
  Ghusl Is Not Required" ^FQH-GHS-06
- **[FQH-GHS-07]** Waking up after a wet dream but finding no wetness, for men or women, does not
  require ghusl. · src: Fiqh-08 "Cases Where Ghusl Is Not Required" ^FQH-GHS-07
- **[FQH-GHS-08]** Inserting an object into the private part without emission does not require
  ghusl. · src: Fiqh-08 "Cases Where Ghusl Is Not Required" ^FQH-GHS-08
- **[FQH-GHS-09]** Intercourse with a non-human object without emission of sperm does not require
  ghusl. · src: Fiqh-08 "Cases Where Ghusl Is Not Required" ^FQH-GHS-09
- **[FQH-GHS-10]** Ghusl is wājib for a person who becomes Muslim while in a state of major ritual
  impurity. · src: Fiqh-08 "Wajib Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-10
- **[FQH-GHS-11]** If a new Muslim was not in a state of major ritual impurity, ghusl is instead
  only recommended (mustaḥabb) for them, not wājib. · src: Fiqh-08 "Wajib Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-11
- **[FQH-GHS-12]** Ghusl is sunnah/mandūb before the Friday (Jumuʿah) prayer. · src: Fiqh-08
  "Sunnah/Mandub Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-12
- **[FQH-GHS-13]** Ghusl is sunnah/mandūb before the two ʿĪd prayers. · src: Fiqh-08
  "Sunnah/Mandub Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-13
- **[FQH-GHS-14]** Ghusl is sunnah/mandūb upon entering the state of pilgrim sanctity (Iḥrām). ·
  src: Fiqh-08 "Sunnah/Mandub Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-14
- **[FQH-GHS-15]** Ghusl is sunnah/mandūb on the Day of ʿArafah. · src: Fiqh-08 "Sunnah/Mandub
  Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-15
- **[FQH-GHS-16]** Ghusl is sunnah/mandūb upon entering Makkah. · src: Fiqh-08 "Sunnah/Mandub
  Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-16
- **[FQH-GHS-17]** Ghusl is sunnah/mandūb upon entering Madīnah. · src: Fiqh-08 "Sunnah/Mandub
  Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-17
- **[FQH-GHS-18]** Ghusl is sunnah/mandūb for visiting the Prophet ﷺ. · src: Fiqh-08
  "Sunnah/Mandub Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-18
- **[FQH-GHS-19]** Farḍ 1 of ghusl is rinsing the entire mouth once. · src: Fiqh-08 "Obligatory
  (Fard) of Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-19
- **[FQH-GHS-20]** Farḍ 2 of ghusl is rinsing the nose up to the bone once. · src: Fiqh-08
  "Obligatory (Fard) of Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-20
- **[FQH-GHS-21]** Farḍ 3 of ghusl is washing whatever of the entire body is possible without
  difficulty or hardship. · src: Fiqh-08 "Obligatory (Fard) of Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-21
- **[FQH-GHS-22]** Making the intention (niyyah) is a sunnah of ghusl. · src: Fiqh-08 "Sunnah
  Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-22
- **[FQH-GHS-23]** Washing the hands up to the wrists at the start is a sunnah of ghusl. · src:
  Fiqh-08 "Sunnah Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-23
- **[FQH-GHS-24]** Washing the private parts (front and rear) is a sunnah of ghusl. · src: Fiqh-08
  "Sunnah Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-24
- **[FQH-GHS-25]** Performing wuḍūʾ, then pouring water over the entire body three times starting
  with the head, is a sunnah of ghusl. · src: Fiqh-08 "Sunnah Ghusl" ^FQH-GHS-25
- **[FQH-GHS-26]** A person with minor ritual impurity cannot pray, touch the Qurʾān, or perform
  ṭawāf, but is permitted to enter a mosque, read the Qurʾān without touching the muṣḥaf, and
  fast; they remain obligated to observe prayer, fasting, and other obligations. · src: Fiqh-08
  "Ruling for Minor Impurity" ^FQH-GHS-26
- **[FQH-GHS-27]** A person with major ritual impurity cannot pray, touch the Qurʾān, perform
  ṭawāf, recite the Qurʾān, or enter a mosque; they can fast, though prayer remains invalid until
  ghusl is performed. · src: Fiqh-08 "Ruling for Major Impurity" ^FQH-GHS-27
- **[FQH-GHS-28]** A menstruating or postnatal-bleeding woman follows the same rulings as major
  ritual impurity, and additionally cannot pray or fast during that time, and it is ḥarām to have
  sexual relations with her during that time. · src: Fiqh-08 "Ruling for Menstruating/Post Natal"
  ^FQH-GHS-28
