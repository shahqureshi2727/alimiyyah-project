# Fiqh-15 — Conditions of Salah

## Topic: Salah Prayer Times  #fiqh/prayer-times

- **[FQH-SLH-24]** The first condition (shart) of Ṣalāh is ritual purity — being free from minor
  impurity (requiring wuḍūʾ) and major impurity (requiring ghusl). · src: Fiqh-15 "Conditions
  (Shurut) — 1. Ritual Purity" ^FQH-SLH-24
- **[FQH-SLH-25]** The second condition of Ṣalāh is cleanliness from physical filth (najāsah),
  which must be absent from the body, clothing, and place of prayer. · src: Fiqh-15 "Conditions
  (Shurut) — 2. Cleanliness from Impurity" ^FQH-SLH-25
- **[FQH-SLH-26]** The third condition of Ṣalāh is covering the ʿawrah: for men this is the area
  from below the navel to below the knees; for women it is the entire body except the face,
  hands, and feet. · src: Fiqh-15 "Conditions (Shurut) — 3. Covering the Awrah" ^FQH-SLH-26
- **[FQH-SLH-27]** The fourth condition of Ṣalāh is facing the qiblah: in Makkah, and able to see
  the Kaʿbah, one faces it directly; elsewhere, one faces the general direction (jihah) of the
  Kaʿbah. · src: Fiqh-15 "Conditions (Shurut) — 4. Facing the Qiblah" ^FQH-SLH-27
- **[FQH-SLH-28]** The fifth condition of Ṣalāh is that its prescribed time has entered — prayer
  is invalid before its appointed time begins, and one must have conviction or reasonable surety
  that the time has entered. · src: Fiqh-15 "Conditions (Shurut) — 5. Entering of Prayer Time"
  ^FQH-SLH-28
- **[FQH-SLH-29]** If someone prays without being reasonably sure that the prayer time has
  entered, the prayer is invalid, even if it turns out to have actually been within the correct
  time. · src: Fiqh-15 "Conditions (Shurut) — 5. Entering of Prayer Time" ^FQH-SLH-29
- **[FQH-SLH-30]** The sixth condition of Ṣalāh is the intention (niyyah), which is located in the
  heart, must be present before the prayer begins, and does not need to be verbalized. · src:
  Fiqh-15 "Conditions (Shurut) — 6. Intention (Niyyah)" ^FQH-SLH-30
- **[FQH-SLH-31]** The seventh condition of Ṣalāh is Takbīrat al-Iḥrām — saying "Allāhu Akbar" to
  begin the prayer, on the tongue such that it is audible to oneself, aligned with the intention,
  and not separated from it by speech or unrelated actions. · src: Fiqh-15 "Conditions (Shurut) —
  7. Takbirat al-Ihram" ^FQH-SLH-31
- **[FQH-SLH-32]** The conditions of Ṣalāh may be relaxed due to valid excuses — e.g. a lack of
  water to remove impurity, the absence of a proper garment to cover the ʿawrah, fear of danger
  (allowing prayer in any safe direction), or illness/inability (allowing prayer facing whatever
  direction is possible). · src: Fiqh-15 "Miscellaneous Rulings" ^FQH-SLH-32
- **[FQH-SLH-33]** If one is uncertain of the qiblah direction, one should make a reasonable
  effort (taḥarrī) to determine it; the prayer is valid even if the chosen direction is later
  proven incorrect. · src: Fiqh-15 "Miscellaneous Rulings — Uncertainty of Qiblah" ^FQH-SLH-33
- **[FQH-SLH-34]** If a mistake in the qiblah direction is discovered during the Ṣalāh itself, one
  must immediately turn toward the correct direction and continue the prayer — there is no need
  to restart it. · src: Fiqh-15 "Miscellaneous Rulings — Uncertainty of Qiblah" ^FQH-SLH-34
