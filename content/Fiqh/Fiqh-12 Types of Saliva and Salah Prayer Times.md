# Fiqh-12 — Types of Saliva; Salaah Prayer Times (Intro)

## Topic: Water Classification  #fiqh/water

- **[FQH-WTR-12]** Water is divided into four categories based on the saliva of an animal or
  person who has drunk from it: pure and purifying, impure, disliked (makrūh), and doubtful —
  each affecting whether the water can be used for wuḍūʾ or drinking. · src: Fiqh-12 "Types of
  Saliva" ^FQH-WTR-12
- **[FQH-WTR-13]** Water remains pure and purifying if only the saliva of a human (even one in
  janābah or a non-Muslim), a horse, or an animal whose meat is ḥalāl has mixed with it. · src:
  Fiqh-12 "Pure and Purifying Saliva" ^FQH-WTR-13
- **[FQH-WTR-14]** Water becomes impure — and cannot be used for wuḍūʾ or drinking except in dire
  necessity — if the saliva of a dog, a pig, or a predatory land animal (e.g. lions, wolves,
  hyenas, apes) has mixed with it. · src: Fiqh-12 "Impure Saliva" ^FQH-WTR-14
- **[FQH-WTR-15]** Water is disliked (makrūh) to use, if other pure water is available, when the
  saliva of a housecat, a free-roaming chicken, a predatory bird (e.g. hawks, falcons), or an
  animal commonly found around homes (e.g. mice, snakes) has mixed with it; if no other water is
  available, it may still be used for purification. · src: Fiqh-12 "Disliked (Makruh) Saliva"
  ^FQH-WTR-15
- **[FQH-WTR-16]** Water whose saliva-purifying status is doubtful — such as that of a mule or
  donkey — requires a person to perform wuḍūʾ with the water, then also perform tayammum, before
  praying. · src: Fiqh-12 "Doubtful Saliva" ^FQH-WTR-16

## Topic: Salah Prayer Times  #fiqh/prayer-times

- **[FQH-SLH-01]** Fajr prayer time begins at the entrance of true dawn and ends right before
  sunrise. · src: Fiqh-12 "Fajr" ^FQH-SLH-01
- **[FQH-SLH-02]** Ẓuhr prayer time begins immediately after midday (zawāl); it ends when the
  shadow of an object is twice its length, or — according to a second opinion — when the shadow
  equals its length, excluding the midday shadow. · src: Fiqh-12 "Zuhr" ^FQH-SLH-02
