# Fiqh-11 — Wiping Over Casts/Bandages; Purification of Filth

## Topic: Casts and Bandages  #fiqh/jabair

- **[FQH-JBR-01]** If a person has a broken or wounded limb, they may wipe over the cast,
  bandage, or dressing covering it, instead of washing that limb, for as long as the injury
  remains. · src: Fiqh-11 "Wiping Over Casts & Bandages" ^FQH-JBR-01
- **[FQH-JBR-02]** Wiping over a cast or bandage is permissible even if it was put on while the
  person was in a state of major or minor ritual impurity. · src: Fiqh-11 "Wiping Over Casts &
  Bandages" ^FQH-JBR-02
- **[FQH-JBR-03]** It is not required to wipe the entire cast or bandage, only part of it — though
  a weaker opinion holds that wiping the entire cast is required. · src: Fiqh-11 "How Much Must
  Be Wiped?" ^FQH-JBR-03
- **[FQH-JBR-04]** It is not necessary to wash any exposed skin visible between gaps in the
  dressing. · src: Fiqh-11 "How Much Must Be Wiped?" ^FQH-JBR-04
- **[FQH-JBR-05]** If a cast or bandage falls off before the injury heals, the wiping already
  performed over it remains valid and the prayers already prayed remain valid — they do not need
  to be repeated. · src: Fiqh-11 "If the Cast Falls Off" ^FQH-JBR-05
- **[FQH-JBR-06]** Wiping over an injured area covered by a cast or bandage counts as washing that
  area for the purposes of wuḍūʾ or ghusl. · src: Fiqh-11 "If the Cast Falls Off" ^FQH-JBR-06
- **[FQH-JBR-07]** If a new cast or bandage replaces an old one that had already been wiped, it is
  not obligatory to wipe the new one again, though doing so is recommended (mustaḥabb). · src:
  Fiqh-11 "Replacing the Cast" ^FQH-JBR-07

## Topic: Najasah (Impurities)  #fiqh/najasah

- **[FQH-NJS-05]** Filth (najāsah) is classified as heavy (mughallaẓah) or light (mukhaffafah);
  heavy filth includes spilled blood, feces, wine (khamr), and semen (manī). · src: Fiqh-11
  "Types of Filth (Najasa)" ^FQH-NJS-05
- **[FQH-NJS-06]** Light filth (mukhaffafah) includes the urine of animals whose meat is ḥalāl,
  the urine of horses, and the droppings of birds whose meat is ḥarām. · src: Fiqh-11 "Types of
  Filth (Najasa)" ^FQH-NJS-06
- **[FQH-NJS-07]** If filth is visible (discernible) on an item, it is purified by removing the
  substance itself, even with only one washing — except for filth that is difficult to remove
  completely. · src: Fiqh-11 "How to Purify Filth" ^FQH-NJS-07
- **[FQH-NJS-08]** If filth is not visible (indiscernible) on an item, it is purified by washing
  and squeezing it three times; if it cannot be squeezed, it is purified by washing it three
  times, waiting each time until the dripping stops. · src: Fiqh-11 "How to Purify Filth"
  ^FQH-NJS-08
- **[FQH-NJS-09]** In prayer, heavy solid filth is excused up to the size of a dirham
  (approximately 4–5g). · src: Fiqh-11 "Excused Amounts in Prayer" ^FQH-NJS-09
- **[FQH-NJS-10]** In prayer, heavy liquid filth is excused up to an area of approximately 3–5cm.
  · src: Fiqh-11 "Excused Amounts in Prayer" ^FQH-NJS-10
- **[FQH-NJS-11]** In prayer, light filth is excused if it covers less than a quarter of the
  garment or body. · src: Fiqh-11 "Excused Amounts in Prayer" ^FQH-NJS-11
- **[FQH-NJS-12]** Filth does not transfer when a damp impure garment touches a dry pure garment
  without dripping onto it. · src: Fiqh-11 "When Filth Does NOT Transfer" ^FQH-NJS-12
- **[FQH-NJS-13]** Filth does not transfer when a wet foot steps on impure ground and no visible
  trace (colour or smell) results. · src: Fiqh-11 "When Filth Does NOT Transfer" ^FQH-NJS-13
- **[FQH-NJS-14]** Filth does not transfer when a damp garment touches impure ground and no
  visible trace results. · src: Fiqh-11 "When Filth Does NOT Transfer" ^FQH-NJS-14
- **[FQH-NJS-15]** Filth does not transfer when sweat moistens a dry impure garment and no trace
  appears. · src: Fiqh-11 "When Filth Does NOT Transfer" ^FQH-NJS-15
- **[FQH-NJS-16]** Water, even used water, is a permissible liquid for cleaning filth. · src:
  Fiqh-11 "Permissible liquids for cleaning" ^FQH-NJS-16
- **[FQH-NJS-17]** Any pure liquid that removes filth — such as rosewater or vinegar — is
  permissible for cleaning it. · src: Fiqh-11 "Permissible liquids for cleaning" ^FQH-NJS-17
- **[FQH-NJS-18]** Chemical transformation (istiḥālah) — such as feces turning into salt or ash —
  renders a substance pure. · src: Fiqh-11 "Special Purification Cases" ^FQH-NJS-18
- **[FQH-NJS-19]** Solid filth on a khuff or sandals is purified by rubbing or scraping it off,
  without needing to wash it. · src: Fiqh-11 "Special Purification Cases" ^FQH-NJS-19
- **[FQH-NJS-20]** A sword or other smooth, non-absorbent surface is purified from filth by wiping
  it. · src: Fiqh-11 "Special Purification Cases" ^FQH-NJS-20
- **[FQH-NJS-21]** Natural ground is purified by drying, provided no trace of the filth remains —
  though such ground remains unusable for tayammum. · src: Fiqh-11 "Special Purification Cases"
  ^FQH-NJS-21
- **[FQH-NJS-22]** Dried semen (manī) is purified by scraping it off. · src: Fiqh-11 "Special
  Purification Cases" ^FQH-NJS-22
- **[FQH-NJS-23]** Items without flowing blood — such as hair, feathers, horn, claw, or bone
  without fat — do not become impure upon the death of the animal. · src: Fiqh-11 "Special
  Purification Cases" ^FQH-NJS-23
- **[FQH-NJS-24]** Tanned hides, except those of pigs and humans, become pure after tanning. · src:
  Fiqh-11 "Special Purification Cases" ^FQH-NJS-24
