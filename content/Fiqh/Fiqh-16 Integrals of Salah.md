# Fiqh-16 — Integrals (Arkan) of Salah

## Topic: Salah Prayer Times  #fiqh/prayer-times

- **[FQH-SLH-35]** `[madhhab-specific]` The first integral (rukn) of Ṣalāh, Takbīr al-Taḥrīmah —
  saying "Allāhu Akbar" to begin the prayer, aligned with the intention and not separated from it
  by speech or unrelated actions — is considered a rukn (integral) by Imam Muḥammad, but a shart
  (condition) by Imam Abū Ḥanīfah and Imam Abū Yūsuf. · src: Fiqh-16 "Integrals (Arkaan) — 1.
  Takbir al-Tahrimah" ^FQH-SLH-35
- **[FQH-SLH-36]** The second integral of Ṣalāh is standing (qiyām) if physically able; it is
  obligatory in farḍ prayers but not obligatory in voluntary (nafl) prayers. · src: Fiqh-16
  "Integrals (Arkaan) — 2. Standing (Qiyam)" ^FQH-SLH-36
- **[FQH-SLH-37]** The third integral of Ṣalāh is recitation (qirāʾah) — reciting at least one
  verse of the Qurʾān — required in the first two rakʿahs of farḍ prayers and in all rakʿahs of
  witr and nafl prayers. · src: Fiqh-16 "Integrals (Arkaan) — 3. Recitation (Qira'ah)"
  ^FQH-SLH-37
- **[FQH-SLH-38]** The follower (muqtadī) praying behind an imam does not himself recite the
  Qurʾān during the prayer. · src: Fiqh-16 "Integrals (Arkaan) — 3. Recitation (Qira'ah)"
  ^FQH-SLH-38
- **[FQH-SLH-39]** The fourth integral of Ṣalāh is bowing (rukūʿ) — bending forward to the extent
  that the hands reach the knees; its perfection is keeping the head level with the back. · src:
  Fiqh-16 "Integrals (Arkaan) — 4. Bowing (Ruku)" ^FQH-SLH-39
- **[FQH-SLH-40]** The fifth integral of Ṣalāh is prostration (sujūd) — prostrating on the
  forehead, both hands, both knees, and having the toes of both feet on the ground. · src:
  Fiqh-16 "Integrals (Arkaan) — 5. Prostrations (Sujud)" ^FQH-SLH-40
- **[FQH-SLH-41]** The sixth integral of Ṣalāh is the final sitting (qaʿdah akhīrah) at the end of
  the prayer, which must last at least the duration of the tashahhud and is necessary before
  concluding the prayer. · src: Fiqh-16 "Integrals (Arkaan) — 6. Final Sitting (Qa'dah Akhirah)"
  ^FQH-SLH-41
- **[FQH-SLH-42]** It is farḍ for the one praying to exit the Ṣalāh through their own action,
  rather than it ending automatically. · src: Fiqh-16 "Ending the prayer" ^FQH-SLH-42
